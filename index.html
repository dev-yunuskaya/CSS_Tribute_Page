Muhammad Ali

"The Greatest of All Time: A Legacy of Unmatched Bravery and Skill"

A Timeline of His Legendary Life

1942: Born as Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. on January 17 in Louisville, Kentucky, USA.

1954: At age 12, he began boxing after his bike was stolen, which led him to Joe E. Martin, a local police officer and boxing coach.

1960: Won a gold medal in the light heavyweight division at the Rome Olympics and turned professional.

1964: Defeated Sonny Liston to become the heavyweight champion of the world. Shortly after, he converted to Islam and changed his name to Muhammad Ali.

1967: Refused to be drafted into the U.S. military during the Vietnam War, citing religious beliefs and opposition to the war. As a result, he was stripped of his title and banned from boxing for several years.

1971: Lost his first professional fight to Joe Frazier in the "Fight of the Century." His conviction for draft evasion was also overturned by the U.S. Supreme Court.

1974: Regained the heavyweight title by defeating George Foreman in the famous "Rumble in the Jungle" fight in Zaire.

1975: Won the legendary "Thrilla in Manila" fight against Joe Frazier.

1978: Lost and regained the heavyweight title against Leon Spinks, becoming the first boxer to win the heavyweight title three times.

1981: Retired from boxing after losing to Trevor Berbick, finishing with a record of 56 wins and 5 losses.

1984: Diagnosed with Parkinson's disease, likely caused by head trauma from his boxing career.

1996: Lit the Olympic flame at the Atlanta Games, symbolizing his enduring legacy.

2016: Passed away on June 3 at the age of 74 due to respiratory complications.
